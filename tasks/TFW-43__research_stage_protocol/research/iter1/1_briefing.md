> Goal: Determine exact Mindset block wording for 4 research stage templates that produces measurably different cognitive modes in AI agents.

## Research Plan

**Gather:**
- Study the review template Mindset pattern (map.md, verify.md, judge.md) — extract the structural formula: role-noun + instruction + Test question
- Research LLM role-play / persona-assignment effects on task performance — what cognitive framing terms actually shift behavior?
- Catalog the 4 research stages' existing cognitive purposes from HL, glossary, conventions (Gather=decompose, Extract=cross-reference, Challenge=eliminate)
- Check TFW naming evidence: D28 (naming > explanation), F3 (naming creates behavior), F18 (different templates = different cognitive modes)
- Candidate dimension: role-noun vocabulary (Explorer vs Scout vs Mapper, Analyst vs Synthesizer, Critic vs Skeptic vs Stress-Tester)

**Extract:**
- Build a Configuration Space: role-noun × instruction-style × Test-question format × guiding-question placement
- Map each configuration against the 4 research stages to see which combinations produce distinct cognitive modes
- Cross-reference with review template patterns — same structure, adapted vocabulary

**Challenge:**
- Stress-test: do any role-nouns overlap in cognitive connotation? (e.g., Explorer ≈ Scout → no differentiation)
- Test question format: "Did I...?" (self-check) vs "Would someone...?" (external perspective) vs imperative "You must..."
- Verify: does moving h1 guiding questions into Mindset block make them redundant or complementary?
